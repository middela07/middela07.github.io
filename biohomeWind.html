Wind Energy

What is Wind Energy?

Wind energy is a source of energy which is generated from the wind with the use of wind turbines, or, windmills.

Pros

Free Fuel

Wind turbines run solely on the wind, so no extra power is needed to run them.

Clean Form of Energy

Since they don’t rely on fossil fuels to run, the only time they will emit carbon into the atmosphere is during manufacturing.

Cons

Dangerous to Wildlife

Flying animal paths may be disrupted by wind turbines, and they can harm animals that get in the way.

Unpredictable

Because of their high dependency on the wind, wind turbines provide different amounts of energy based on the weather, which means they can fail to provide enough energy when needed.

Applications

Farms

Some windmills generate heat, while others can pump water for agriculture or plantations.

Homes

You can install a small wind turbine for your house, which would significantly help produce enough electricity to reduce your electric bills.

Costs

Installment

Off-grid systems would not directly connect to your home, and costs from $15,000 to $50,000+ per mile, or $10,117 per the amount of kW it can produce.

Reduce Bills

An average home uses about 911 kW hours per month. A small wind turbine could generate 300 KW hours per month, with a 14 mph average year round. You could save ⅓ of your electric bill!
